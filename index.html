Twenty years of watching what works, and what doesn't

I've seen what works at enterprise scale. What fails spectacularly. And what businesses of every size actually need.

A pattern shows up everywhere I've worked. Teams spending twenty-plus hours a week in Excel doing work that should be automated. Leaders chasing AI before fixing the digital foundation underneath it. Operations that can't scale without hiring because their processes were never built to scale.

The idea I keep coming back to is what I call the Missing Middle: the $10,000 to $100,000 improvements that leadership often overlooks as not strategic enough.

That's the lens I bring to any team or project: fix the digital foundation before layering AI on top of it, and look for the improvements that are too small for a boardroom deck but too big to keep ignoring.
